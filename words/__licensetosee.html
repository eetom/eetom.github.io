Photography is license to see

October 26th, 2023
Bellevue, WA

I like taking photos with my phone. It's a liberating feeling when I'm just trying to capture a memory meant to be shared via Google photos and sent to relatives over iMessage. The photos are quick and snappy and I take many at a time just cuz most of them would probably end up a bit off center or blurry (kids tend to move pretty fast and not hold still). I also enjoy taking photos with my film cameras, there's a bit more hesitency when i'm taking photos on film with a limited amount of shots per roll. There's also many different factors that one cannot fully prepare for such as sudden lighting changes, something might have not been mechanically activated in the camera correctly, or even just old expired film doing what old expired film does. Neither digital nor anaolog is a uniquley "better" or more "pure" form of photography. I think people overthink these things too much and it's fine if they prefer one over the other, but what matters to me is that both have the 2 primary qualities of photography, the "act" of taking a photo and the "result" from act. I like takining photos. I feel no matter if it's a phone photo or film photo I put a little bit of me into each photo.
