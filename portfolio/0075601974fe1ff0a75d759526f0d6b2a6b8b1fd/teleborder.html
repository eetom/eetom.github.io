01. Website

Teleborder's mission was to help both companies and new hires navigate the complexities of working in the US. Customers would work directly with our in-house lawyers who would help manage cases and answer any questions along the way.

The first touchpoint that a customer would have with Teleborder is the website. It's here that they learn about the company's mission and services to help companies allocate new hires through the complicated work visa system. It was important that we clearly explain the process and build trust.

We designed the website to include many real examples of testimonials and screenshots. The testimonials and social proof assured that we were effective and achieved results. The screenshots showed how the features and services on our platform worked.

One experiment we conducted was launching a landing page where potential workers looking to work in the US could receive a summary report of the process and difficulty of their case. This could be used as a starting point to have a discussion with potential employers. The website was promoted through the Teleborder social media accounts and quickly became a viral hit.

02. Branding

As part of the early design work, we rebranded from the early designs and logos they had while at Y Combinator. We wanted to build an extendable brand that could be used both online and offline.

I chose simple colors and patterns. We also had many business cards and t-shirts made. One of the best compliments I received was that many of the team wore their t-shirts outside of work to this day because "it didn't look like a work shirt."

I also worked alongside the front-end engineers to build an early version of a design system with standardized colors and fonts.

03. Platform

Once a customer chooses to work with us, the main interaction would take place on the platform. This is where we had to do the most research and be creative with our solutions.

One of the key takeaways we had from our customer interviews was that they didn't necessarily want more explanations and data. They were primarily concerned about status and if they needed to take any action. We worked to keep the number of forms as light as possible and focused on giving users updates and progress reports when needed.

On the other side of the platform where our team would manage the cases, we built in easy access to tools and allowed for custom changes. We spent time talking and understanding the pains of our team about how often cases would change and they would want an interface to aid them in making those changes.

One of my favorite pages we added for the customer-facing platform view was the page when they were done filling things out. We used a relaxing beach background image and let them know that we would take care of everything and update them if we needed anything. This was the page that received the most positive feedback from our customers.
