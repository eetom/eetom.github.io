01. Version 0.5

Originally, the team had worked with a few different options to do the management and updating of program data. They had tried out things like keeping some records in Notion and backend tools like Retool. When those options were't working we replaced those with a rudimentary admin panel pretty similar to the one built in Retool.

This is the homepage of the Admin panel version 0.5. While it primarily utilizes Retool functionalities through links, the team had begun to integrating native features like the program status tracker on the left. This addition empowers our team to monitor the program funnel directly within the Admin panel, streamlining workflow and improving operational efficiency.

To help save time and get started the engineers implemented the data tables using the Signature UI design system. However navigation and responsiveness were not factored in along with other design details.

While the initial versions of our detail pages relied on a spreadsheet-like format leveraging Retool tables, this approach, while straightforward, presented some limitations. Navigating these 'data grids' often required users to spend significant time searching for specific information. The sheer volume of data could be overwhelming, making it difficult to identify key details quickly.

02. Research & testing

To build a more full functioning and well designed admin panel. We spent time talking with the users, the Ledger teams who would be using it to do their day-to-day work.

We interviewed and summed up various metrics and data points that would be useful. Our goal for the new home page would be a dashboard that could give the users a quick sense of what the situation was and what needed attention.

Different users might have different numbers and statistics they wanted to see so we also thought about customization and personalization.

In collaboration with the Product Manager, we conducted a user survey to gather valuable insights. By analyzing the results, we identified recurring themes within the user feedback. This focus on user "wins" and pain points helped us prioritize improvements that would have the greatest positive impact on the user experience.

Following the user research, the PM and I collaborated to develop super low-fidelity wireframes. These initial layouts served two key purposes. First, they provided a foundation for discussions with stakeholders, allowing us to explore different design directions and gather feedback. Secondly, sharing these wireframes with engineers facilitated early identification of potential technical challenges. This proactive approach enabled us to prioritize functionalities and plan for potential roadblocks, ensuring a smoother development process down the line.

I based a lot of my investigative work looking through different designs of comparative data dashboards and what made them work. Some things that really excited me was having some level of customization and personalization such as having your own dashboard set up to how you would best work.

It was also important to see the users' path thoughtout the admin platform. I did this by mapping out the various flows a real user might have to click and flow through the current admin v0.5 to better understand where our navigation was failing us.

I also started mapping the comments and feedback directly onto the pages to better keep track of what we needed to fix and improve.

03. Design

After many rounds of revisions and updates, here is the v1.0 of the Admin portal. The main layout has now a consistent side bar menu. The menu has the most used "All programs" at the top allowing for easy and frequent access. There is room on the side bar for future items such as "My settings" or "BDX consolidation".

In the footer of the app, we added in a section for company frequented links. This would be useful in getting the team to start using and visiting key pages such as helping with testing on our staging site or even giving feedback on our designs in Figma.

The main page is now fully a data dashboard. We moved all the links to the side and categorized it by user. This would help as an interim step for some of the personalization we wanted to put in. There is still an "All" under tools to see all of them.

The data is arranged by top priority when the Ledger team is working on risk origination. The are able to see the where the programs are in the process and adjust their tasks to address anything that most needed their attention.

The tool pages and detail pages got cleaned up and extra details such as empty states with clear messaging to help with new users who aren't familiar with the system.

The full details pages have reworked some of the navigation and cleaned up the hirearchy for the program details. The program details section is hidden but still accessible when needed. We've opted to keep a majority of the tables as from before and will make changes to those at a later date.

There was also a few other UI personalizations we wanted to hold until we added some new features. A lot of the feedback we got after we had launched the is first big update on the admin platform has been very positive. The number of our team visiting the admin platform has greatly increased over the first first months of launch and we can slowly transition most of the program management features over.
