01. Background

Many of the teams across Ledger used many tools such as google docs, notion, and eventually Retool to manage customer data enter by different teams at different touch points. A good amount of time is spent by different teams transcribing data from one source into their own preferred program.

Diverse data formats led to cumbersome data sharing among teams, prolonging customer sales onboarding to several months.

My aim was to unify teams along the customer journey rather than through team hand-offs points, ensuring alignment across multiple teams by the measurement of sale cycle times.

This solution would be to address our internal teams that interact with customers and customer data on a regular basis. These team members range from Sales, Program managers, Underwriters, and Fund Managers. Each of these teams work along an Managing General Agent (MGA) customer teams who would send data while our team evaulates the potential deal to be brokered.

02. Research + Audit

To get a better understanding of the challenges of unifying the customer data I needed to understand the pain points, goals, and expectations.

  • Exploration of the v0.5 platform
  • Talking to each of the teams involved via survey and interviews
  • Mapping out the jobs to be done by each team against the current platform

03. Quick wins + Lofi

To action upon the findings, I prioritized the data to form a strategy forward. Because we were working with a internal customer, this allow us to update continously with a tight cycle of feedback from partners in other teams. While we made small improvements in the 0.5 platform, we iterated on designs for the brand new version.

  • What are quick wins for the current platform?
  • What are the scope and scale bounds for v1.0?

04. Design + Hand-off

Working with the PM and engineers to bring designs to life.

  • Lofi mockups to measure against requirements.
  • Review and update key navigational updates.
  • Hand-off and desiging future-proof components.

05. Outcomes

The first release of the new internal admin in September sparked a 50% increase in internal traffic, despite being incomplete. We also noticed shorter sales cycles, down to weeks between customer steps. As enthusiasm grew, I started sharing plans for more metrics and analytics.
